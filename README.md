## 1. Problem Statement

Organizations often struggle to monitor sales, profit, and performance across multiple countries, products, and customer segments using raw data alone. The lack of a centralized and interactive view makes it difficult to identify trends, compare performance, and make data-driven business decisions.

This project addresses that problem by creating an **interactive financial analysis dashboard in Excel** that provides clear insights into sales, profit, and unit performance across different dimensions.



## 2. Objectives

* To analyze **financial performance** across countries, products, and segments
* To track **gross sales, profit, and units sold** over time
* To identify **top-performing products, markets, and customer segments**
* To enable **interactive analysis** using slicers and filters
* To support **data-driven business decision-making**



## 3. Data Overview

The dataset used for this dashboard is sourced from the **Excel file pinned above** and contains financial transaction-level data.

### Key attributes in the dataset:

* **Date** (Year, Month)
* **Country** (Canada, France, Germany, Mexico, USA)
* **Product** (Amarilla, Carretera, Montana, Paseo, Velo, VTT)
* **Segment** (Channel Partners, Enterprise, Government, Midmarket, Small Business)
* **Units Sold**
* **Gross Sales**
* **Profit**

This data enables multi-dimensional financial analysis across **time, geography, product, and customer segments**.

## 4. Data Preparation and Calculation

* Cleaned the dataset by ensuring consistent date formats and categorical values
* Created **calculated fields** such as:

  * Total Gross Sales
  * Total Profit
  * Total Units Sold
* Used **Pivot Tables** to aggregate data by:

  * Country
  * Product
  * Segment
  * Month and Year
* Applied **Excel slicers and timeline filters** for dynamic data interaction

## 6. Observations and Key Insights

* **Government and Enterprise segments** contribute the highest number of units sold
* Certain products (such as **Paseo and VTT**) generate significantly higher profits
* **October and November** show peak sales, indicating possible seasonal demand
* Sales are well-distributed across countries, with some markets consistently outperforming others
* Monthly trends reveal fluctuations that can guide forecasting and inventory planning



## 7. Business Recommendations

* Focus marketing and sales efforts on **high-profit products**
* Strengthen engagement with **Government and Enterprise segments**
* Prepare inventory and promotions ahead of **peak sales months**
* Expand strategies in **high-performing countries** while improving underperforming regions
* Use monthly trends to support **better financial forecasting**
